    TibSchol {% translate "Database" %}

    The Dawn of Tibetan Buddhist Scholasticism (11th–13th century)

    TibSchol explores the formative phase of the Tibetan Buddhist scholastic tradition in the 11th to 13th century—an exceptionally active period of Tibetan scholarly creativity.

    Who were the individuals involved in the early Tibetan scholastic tradition? What were the connections between them and with enclaves of learning? What works did they compose, teach, and study? What was the range of diffusion and the mode of circulation of these works?

    This prosopographical database contains the data collected by the TibSchol project team with the aim of answering such questions—and more—in order to contribute to a more comprehensive and dynamic picture of the Tibetan scholastic landscape, with a focus on the 11th to 13th centuries. More generally, it seeks to offer a better-grounded and more nuanced understanding of Tibetan intellectual history.

    The database includes information on persons, works, instances of works (particularly manuscript versions), and places, along with supporting primary and secondary sources that document the relationships between these entities. Beginning with instances of works from the recently published bKa’ gdams gsung ’bum series and their authors, the database has expanded to incorporate further corpora and sources, bringing lesser-known entities into focus.

    TibSchol aims to reconstruct both personal and intellectual networks. The latter are uncovered through intertextual references, documented in the database via textual excerpts from original sources.

    The project TibSchol is hosted at the Institute for the Cultural and Intellectual History of Asia at the Austrian Academy of Sciences. The database was developed with the technical expertise of the ACDH, using the Austrian Prosopographical/Biographical Information System (APIS) platform to accommodate TibSchol’s data model. It is linked to the Zotero group library “TibSchol”, which provides metadata for cited sources and relation support, and to a TEI repository of textual excerpts, both of which are also accessible independently.

    Integration with other databases in the field of Tibetan and Buddhist studies is facilitated through links to corresponding entities on the Buddhist Digital Archives (BUDA) of the Buddhist Digital Resource Center (BDRC), and, for Indian canonical Buddhist works, to the Resources for Kanjur and Tanjur Studies (rKTs) database.

    TibSchol is committed to making its research results openly accessible.

    The dataset contained in the database is placed under the Creative Commons license CC0, at the exception of Comments (in pages related to Person, Work, Instance, Place) and Item description (in Instance pages), which are placed under the Creative Common license CC BY 4.0.

    Dates for Persons are, unless they are approximated (before/after) or it is indicated otherwise in the Comments, those provided by the Buddhist Digital Resource Center under CC0 license at the URL indicated under “External links.”

    For Indic Works and their author, we used the title and name provided by the rKTs project. More details can be obtained on these works by following the external link to rKTs.

    Coordinates for Places are, unless indicated otherwise in the Comments, those provided by the Buddhist Digital Resource Center under CC0 license at the URL indicated under “External links.”

    Please note and respect the copyright regulations specific to other resources referred to in the database, such as publications and online resources.

    TibSchol database does not contain metadata on natural persons.

    Text excerpts (text_snippet) are from works in the Public Domain. Related TEI files that contain these excerpts, hosted at TibSchol-TEI-Library, are distributed under a Creative Common CC BY-SA 4.0 License.
