Overview

This prosopographical database contains data collected by the TibSchol project team in its exploration of the formative phase of the Tibetan Buddhist scholastic tradition. Beyond the focus on scholastic works and related activities of composition and learning in the 11th to 13th c., data has been included that concerns later developments of individual textual traditions, non-scholastic texts, as well as scholars that were not involved in scholastic activities but operate as links enabling to put on the maps lesser-known figures. Beginning with the survey of manuscripts from the recently published bKa’ gdams gsung ’bum series, the database has expanded to incorporate further corpora and sources, including also records of lost works, to offer a more nuanced understanding of scholars’ endeavors in all their many facets. We strove to provide support for all relations between entities, which are documented in the database via textual excerpts from original sources (sored in our TEI repository) as well as secondary literature (listed in TibSchol Zotero group library).

Not all entries in the database have received the same amount of attention. Information in the database will keep being enhanced in the future and is supplemented by links to other databases in the field of Tibetan and Buddhist studies. Provided in particular are links the rich resources of the Buddhist Digital Archives (BUDA) of the Buddhist Digital Resource Center (BDRC); for epistemological works and their authors to EAST “Epistemology and Argumentation in South Asia and Tibet”; and more generally for all Indian Buddhist canonical works, to the Resources for Kanjur and Tanjur Studies (rKTs) database.
